Overview of MATH 3022 Algebra II

Description

This is the second introductory course on the structures of Algebra, embracing Groups, Rings and Fields and other algebraic topics. The focus of this course is Ring Theory and Fields. We will study the general concepts of rings: homomorphisms, ideals, integral domains, fields, and vector spaces. This general theory is studied hand-in-hand with different types of rings in detail, e.g. commutative rings, rings with identities, integral domains, division rings, and fields.

Real Prerequisites

This is a theoretical, proof-based course. Familiarity with concepts from linear algebra is helpful, but it is not enough to follow this course effectively. Proficiency in carrying out methods of proof and fluency in handling set notation and operations (writing proofs by induction and contradiction and working with abstract objects and collections of sets) is essential. A good grasp of MATH 1200 Problems, Conjectures, and Proofs and MATH 3021 Algebra I is an asset for assimilating the material of this course.

Academic honestly in homework submissions

You are allowed to discuss your work on the homework assignments with your classmates. However, the submitted solutions to these assignments must be in your own words. Submitting common solutions with only notational variation or sentence-for-sentence paraphrasing is considered cheating. Furthermore, consulting solutions from third-party sites, such as Chegg, is not allowed. A safe approach is first to discuss the problems and their solutions with your classmates and then write your submission separately without consulting each other's notes. Breach of academic honesty will be reported and handled according to the policy of the University.

Final Exam (45% of total score)

A cumulative final exam will be given during the final examination period. It will be a three-hour written exam that can only be taken in-person.

Late or Missed Assignments

  • You will be allowed up to two late submissions of the assignments, without giving reasons. Late submissions are due at the start of the next lecture after the due date. (For the last assignment, late submission is due at 9:30 a.m. on April 11.)
  • You need provide legitimate reasons for missed work asap. Examples of legitimate reasons include prolonged and serious sickness, personal/family emergency, religious observance.
  • I will start adding questions to an assignment about three weeks prior to the due date and all questions will be posted at least one week prior the due date, so being sick for the couple of days before the due date does not count as legitimate reason for missing the assignment.
  • Contact me asap if you miss work with legitimate reasons. I will ask you to complete the work without grades and then transfer the weight of the missed work to the final exam.
  • Upon missing more than one assessment, you are required to meet with the instructor and present a plan to catch up with the work.
  • For missed final examination, students need to submit the Deferred Standing Agreement Form, no later than one week after the examination. Upon approval of deferred standing, the student will write the final exam at the Common Deferred Examination scheduled by the department.
